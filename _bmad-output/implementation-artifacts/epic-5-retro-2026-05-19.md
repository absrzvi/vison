## What Went Well

- **Throughput.** All 4 stories landed in a single day.

## What Hurt

- **Spec gaps.** Unlike Epic 2 (DD-001), Epic 5 had no equivalent design doc. Implementation drifted from intent → 29 review patches.
- **ISO timestamp drift across 5-1/5-2/5-3.** Story 5-4 was retroactive cleanup that should not have been necessary. It signals each of 5-1/5-2/5-3 made an independent timestamp decision without a shared contract.

## Root Cause

> **No design spec = drift.** Epic 2 worked because DD-001 was the single source of truth. Epic 5 lacked one. Each story made local decisions, each local decision drifted from the next story's local decision, and CR became the place where consistency was negotiated. That's the wrong forum — by the time CR finds it, the patch is in someone's PR, not in the design.

This is the same pattern as Epic 1's root cause (contract exists in docs but not enforced) — at a different layer. Epic 1: types weren't enforced at CI. Epic 5: visual/data contracts weren't enforced at design.

## Previous Retro Follow-Through

Epic 2's action items (B1–B3: Playwright in story 1, interaction-edges checklist, dev-must-script-interaction) were filed *after* Epic 5 ran. Epic 5 did not benefit from them. Patch density supports the theory that B1–B3 would have helped — and that the equivalent for Epic 5 is **design doc as gate** (already endorsed below).

## Key Insight

> Epic 5 proved you can ship fast without a design doc, but the cost shows up in CR. The DD-001 pattern from Epic 2 is not optional polish — it's the mechanism that keeps patch density low. Promote it from "what we did once" to "what we always do for UI epics."

## Cross-Epic Pattern (3 retros in)

Three retros, three flavors of the **same** root cause:

- **Epic 1:** types exist in code, not enforced in CI → consumers violate
- **Epic 2:** design spec exists, doesn't cover interaction edges → CR finds them
- **Epic 5:** no design spec at all → CR negotiates consistency

**The meta-action:** every contract (type, design, timestamp, envelope) must be enforced at the **earliest** layer possible — CI for code, design doc for UI, validator for data. CR is the wrong place to discover contract violations.
