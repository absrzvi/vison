## What Went Well

- **Event envelope + EventType taxonomy held up.** ADR-1 / ADR-5 design has carried Epics 2, 3, and 5 to done without re-design. The single source of truth in `shared/events/types.py` is the load-bearing decision of the epic.

## What Hurt

- **Missing EventTypes discovered late.** Commit `f6d377c` added 6 missing EventTypes + payloads only after rtsp-ingest (Epic 4) violated the envelope. Foundation shipped incomplete; the gap was found by a downstream consumer, not by the foundation tests.
- **MQTT → REST pivot mid-epic.** Story numbering (missing 1-6) reflects abandoned MQTT/Mosquitto/TimescaleDB work. Decision was correct but cost was unrecorded.
- **Coverage/CI gates added late.** NFR12 (80% coverage) was not enforced from Story 1-1. Tech debt accrued silently across the epic.
- **rtsp-ingest envelope ADR violations.** Foundation did not enforce the envelope contract — consumers could violate ADR-1 at runtime instead of CI-time.

**Root cause (single thread connecting all four):** The foundation epic shipped types and schemas but **did not ship enforcement**. Contracts existed in markdown (ADRs) and Pydantic models, but nothing failed CI when a consumer violated them. Downstream epics paid the cost.

## Key Insight

> A foundation epic's deliverable isn't the types — it's the **contract enforcement**. Pydantic models that aren't checked in CI are documentation, not infrastructure.
