# Persona: Conductor Conrad — Primary 👥

**Phase:** 2 — Trigger Map
**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Goal Connection:** Goals 1, 2, 3

---

## Who Conrad Is

Conrad is a senior ÖBB train conductor on an intercity route. 12 years in the role, respected by colleagues, responsible for passenger safety and comfort across a 10-coach train. He carries a mobile handheld, manages a team of two junior staff on busier services, and is the last line of defence before departure.

---

## Psychological Profile

Conrad operates in **constant context-switching mode** — one moment managing a passenger complaint, the next responding to a door alarm, the next directing boarding at a busy platform stop. His mental bandwidth is finite and genuinely valuable. He resents tools that waste it.

He is **deeply sceptical of new technology** introduced by management without consultation. He has seen too many "innovation projects" that created more work than they saved. His default position on any new system is suspicion until proved otherwise. He will adopt — but only if adoption is fast and the payoff is immediate and obvious.

Conrad has a **strong professional identity**. He takes pride in running a smooth, on-time service. A delayed departure because he missed a door obstruction stings — not just operationally, but personally. He holds himself to a higher standard than the job description requires.

---

## Internal State

When Conrad thinks about the current state of passenger management, he feels **exhausted but in control** — he knows how to do his job, but he also knows he is doing it harder than he should have to. He doesn't know what he's missing because he can't be in all coaches at once. That uncertainty sits quietly in the background: *what happened in coach 7 while I was in coach 2?*

---

## Usage Context

**Access:** Conrad receives a device with the Conductor App pre-installed. Onboarding happens during a 30-minute crew briefing before first use.

**Emotional State:** Busy, mildly resistant, will give it 10 minutes. If it doesn't deliver value within the first journey, it becomes another thing he ignores.

**Behaviour Pattern:** Checks the app at key moments — departure prep, station approaches, alert notifications. Does not browse. Needs the most important thing on the screen immediately, with no navigation required.

**Decision Criteria:** Does it tell me something I didn't already know, faster than I could find it myself? Does it make me look better at my job, or does it make my job harder?

**Success Outcome:** Conrad taps one alert, sees which coach, walks straight there. Problem resolved before departure. Train leaves on time. He didn't have to walk the whole train.

---

## Driving Forces

### Positive Drivers ✅

1. **Know the whole train at a glance** — wants a single-screen summary of what's happening in every coach simultaneously, something physically impossible without AI — so he can prioritise his movement and attention without guesswork
2. **Act before problems escalate** — wants to be notified of unattended luggage, door obstructions, and accessibility needs before they become incidents, preserving his professional reputation and the train's on-time record
3. **Feel in control during high-stress departures** — peak stress is the 2-minute window before scheduled departure; wants certainty that there are no outstanding issues rather than the current anxious uncertainty of incomplete manual checks
4. **Look authoritative to passengers** — when Conrad approaches a coach problem already knowing what it is, he appears competent and in command; this matters to him personally and professionally
5. **Spend less time walking, more time managing** — physical traversal of the train is exhausting and time-inefficient; wants to route himself purposefully to where he is actually needed

### Negative Drivers ❌

1. **Fear missing a safety-critical incident** because he was in the wrong part of the train — a wheelchair user needing a ramp, a bag blocking a door, a passenger in distress — the thought that something serious happened and he didn't know is the highest-anxiety scenario in his working life
2. **Fear being blamed for a delayed departure** he could have prevented — delay attribution is visible and logged; Conrad knows that "I didn't see it" is not a defence, and the current system gives him no better answer
3. **Frustration with alert overload** — past experience with poorly configured systems that cry wolf constantly; he will mute or ignore any system that generates false positives at scale
4. **Fear of looking incompetent in front of passengers** when something goes wrong publicly — a blocked door, a passenger shouting, a missed accessibility need — all of these play out in front of people who judge immediately
5. **Resentment of tools that require training** — every minute spent learning a new system is a minute not doing his job; he wants zero learning curve or something very close to it
